# The Google Deal — and the BD Architecture Around It

## ⚠️ Headline correction (binding)

Bill, verbatim intent: **"sold to Google" is not the right framing.** It was a
first-of-its-kind *partnership* — working across Google divisions, aligning
incentives, built through proof of commitment and business incentives. A BD
partnerships deal unseen in the Southeast Asian market that built Dreamship's
brand, built Google's brand, and gave merchants access.

## What Bill said (cleaned)

**Origin.** Bill was in the first contact, reached through an executive
connection who is a director. Dreamship had built a **trademark & IP checker
on Google Vision technology**. What Google wanted: access to an untapped
market in a way that protected the Google brand. What Dreamship brought:
**high-volume merchants — verified, validated — driving significant revenue
for Google through the Dreamship trust layer.**

**The room.** The deal never almost died. Bill's contribution: a message that
was passionate, persuasive, and committed — and **organizing the incentives
so it was win-win-win**: for Dreamship, for Google, for the merchants.

**The flywheel.** The Google deal was followed by partnerships with **Stripe,
PayPal, Payoneer, Meta**, and other players who wanted the same access.
Bill's own read: *"there's something there about creating business development
deals that are multi-party and complex and have the right incentives aligned."*
← This is the positioning thesis for the whole case study.

**The 11x year.** First full year of business; a little COVID tailwind; mostly
capitalizing on the opportunity that presented itself — took in orders,
figured out supply routes, met the demand, and turned it into a trusted source
for reliable shipping. Not one decision: a set of decisions by a highly
capable team, led by Bill.

**The Microsoft deal — from the chemo chair.** During aggressive chemo at
**Duke hospital**, Bill used his connections to get into Microsoft just as it
was starting to work with ChatGPT. He built a **rev-share / go-to-market
partnership** giving Dreamship merchants access to ChatGPT via Bing — cut an
aggressive deal that got meaningful money from Microsoft to push Bing
(merchants cared about the ChatGPT capability, not Bing per se), negotiated
with senior Microsoft people, and graduated Dreamship into a higher partner
tier on volume. *Literally from the hospital, during treatment.*
