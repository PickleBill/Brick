# Deal Case-Study Library

Reusable STAR-format case studies for résumé, interviews, the terminal, and a deal-sheet one-pager. Economics tagged blur/⚠️ — confirm publishability (OPEN-Q #6).

## 1. The Google Partnership (crown jewel) — public; economics blur
- **Situation:** A sub-one-year-old startup (Dreamship) with no logo and no track record. Google wanted untapped-market access while protecting the Google brand.
- **Task:** Open a first-of-its-kind cross-division Google partnership — an 8-figure ad-spend channel — for verified merchants.
- **Action:** Built an AI trademark/IP-compliance checker on **Google Vision** to solve *Google's* trust problem; carried it through 5–6 internal Google teams; **organized the incentives so every party won** (Google, the merchants, Dreamship). First contact via an executive (a director); Bill in the room from the start.
- **Result:** Partnership ran 5+ years as a major channel; **Stripe, PayPal, Payoneer, and Meta then wanted the same access.** Built Dreamship's brand and Google's brand simultaneously.
- **GTM lesson / pull-quote:** *"We didn't sell to Google. We built something Google hadn't done before — and organized the incentives so everyone in the room won."*
- **Why it lands for AI labs:** complex, multi-party, multi-stakeholder distribution deal = exactly the partnerships motion labs are hiring for; and the warmest network asset Bill has (reactivate dormant Google contacts).

## 2. The Microsoft Deal — From the Chemo Chair — public (health publicity per Bill); economics ⚠️
- **Situation:** Microsoft was beginning its ChatGPT/Bing push; Dreamship merchants needed differentiated distribution. Bill was in aggressive chemo at Duke hospital.
- **Task:** Land a rev-share/GTM partnership giving Dreamship merchants ChatGPT access via Bing.
- **Action:** Negotiated with senior Microsoft people **from the hospital, mid-treatment**; cut a deal with meaningful Microsoft dollars to push Bing; earned a higher partner tier on volume.
- **Result:** Graduated Dreamship to a higher Microsoft partner tier; merchant access to a new AI-driven channel.
- **GTM lesson / pull-quote:** *"I cut the Microsoft deal from a chair at Duke hospital, mid-chemo."* — operate-through-anything proof; humanizes without leading with illness.

## 3. Dreamship 0→$35M, 11x in a year — public; economics blur
- **Situation:** Early-stage fulfillment platform, first full year of business + a COVID tailwind.
- **Task:** Meet exploding demand and turn it into a durable, trusted shipping source.
- **Action:** Took orders, figured out supply routes, met demand, built the trust layer; not one decision — a *set* of decisions by a capable team Bill led. Later authored "How We Win" to move from scattered bets to sharper partner selection + commercial measurement.
- **Result:** **$35M+ revenue, 11x in a single year**, 323% CAGR 2019–21, 2.3M+ units, 21 fulfillment locations/6 countries. Profitable 4+/8 years.
- **GTM lesson:** capitalize on a market window with operating discipline, not heroics; build the trust layer that compounds into more partnerships.

## 4. IBM / Netezza — Enterprise seven-figure motion — public; some ⚠️
- **Situation:** Big-data/analytics + early cognitive-systems enterprise sales.
- **Action:** Consultative selling, technical-buyer education, internal IBM stakeholder management.
- **Result:** Structured seven-figure deals; Zillow, Avalara; ⚠️ 8-figure McKesson contribution; >100% quota.
- **GTM lesson:** the enterprise-credibility spine — Bill can run a complex, multi-stakeholder enterprise cycle, which de-risks him for any lab's enterprise GTM.
