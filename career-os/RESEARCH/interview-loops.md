# GTM/Partnerships Interview Loops at AI Labs (2025–2026)

Sourced from Glassdoor/Blind/Levels.fyi aggregators + interview-prep platforms. NDAs limit specifics; treat detailed rubrics as directional. Confidence inline.

## Anthropic (best-documented)
- **Structure:** 2 screens + 4 onsite rounds (+ reference). ~3–5 weeks. Recruiter (30m) → HM (60m, deal mechanics/performance) → take-home (5–7 days) → onsite "Super Day" (5 consecutive: culture, negotiation, teamwork, leadership) → references. [strong]
- **GTM/AE exercise:** 2-page memo synthesizing a customer/partnership scenario + mapping value props; **presentation simulating a client/partner meeting** (position Claude safety/steerability/context window vs partner pain); vertical deep-dive (regulatory + buyer personas). Rubric prizes **clear narrative > flashy design**; "why this partner / what does Anthropic unlock." [strong] dataford.io
- **Bars:** vertical expertise; collaboration (orchestrate SE/product/research, not heroics); technical fluency (API, model variants, safety-as-advantage); written clarity; **genuine mission/safety conviction** (dedicated "Mission & Values" round, no right answers). [strong] theundercoverrecruiter.com, interviewquery
- Difficulty ~2.8–4.2/5 depending on source; writing weighted heavily, presentation less.

## OpenAI
- **Structure:** resume screen → phone/video → skills test → 4–6 final rounds over 1–2 days. ~32 days avg. [strong]
- **AE/Sales:** mini-project/work sample + HM review; scenario-based (live discovery, territory planning, deal qualification). Competencies: enterprise deal strategy; technical AI fluency (cost/latency/privacy); discovery & value engineering (ROI quantification); operating cadence/forecasting; values & integrity. Culture: "intense and scrappy," AGI focus; "research discussion" round (engage unfamiliar papers). [strong] dataford.io
- Cultural fit determines the offer once technical bar is cleared.

## Google DeepMind
- 5–7 rounds over 6–10 weeks (recruiter → HM → 1–2 technical → 4–5 onsite). GTM-specific structure not publicly detailed (commercialization runs via Google Cloud). **2026 policy: AI tools prohibited/limited in technical rounds.** [strong general; moderate GTM]

## Mistral / Cohere / xAI
- Mistral: building US GTM/BD from scratch; 170+ open roles; partner-led (Accenture deal). Cohere: ~500 ppl, enterprise-first; ~15% layoff 2025. xAI: thin public process. GTM-specific loops not documented. [low-moderate]

## Cross-lab generalized GTM loop
1. Recruiter screen (background, motivation, comp)
2. HM chat (deal mechanics, account depth, performance)
3. Take-home/case (2–3pg memo + presentation/strategic doc)
4. 2–3 behavioral/cross-functional rounds (deal execution, cross-team orchestration, discovery)
5. Values/culture round (mission alignment — explicit at Anthropic)
6. Sometimes leadership/"bar raiser"

**Common case types:** "Design a GTM strategy to launch [product] with [Company X] in [Vertical Y]"; market sizing & channel; deal negotiation role-play; competitive positioning vs OpenAI/Anthropic; revenue impact & KPI modeling. [strong]
**Common eval criteria:** customer/partner empathy; cross-functional collaboration; strategic thinking; execution discipline; mission alignment; clear written+verbal storytelling. [strong]

| | Anthropic | OpenAI |

| Case style | Narrative memo + presentation, "why us" | Mini-project, deal scenario, territory |

## What this means for Bill
- **Prep the written memo discipline now** — both labs grade crisp, data-backed, fluff-free prose. His FACTS-ledger habit maps perfectly.
- **Build the "why this partner / what we unlock" muscle** with a real case (e.g., a Claude × [vertical] partnership thesis).
- **Lead with collaboration, not lone-wolf closing** — Anthropic explicitly screens against sharp elbows; frame the Google deal as win-win-win orchestration (which it was).
- **Do genuine mission/safety homework** — read Constitutional AI / RSP; form a real POV. This is gradeable and fakeable-detectable.
- **Expect a customer-conversation simulation** (Applied AI) — rehearse discovery + value-mapping for 2–3 target verticals.
