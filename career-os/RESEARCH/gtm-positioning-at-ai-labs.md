# GTM/Partnerships Hiring at Frontier AI Labs (2025–2026)

How senior GTM/partnerships candidates actually win at frontier labs and top AI startups. Confidence flags: **[strong]** / **[moderate]** / **[speculation]**. Sources inline.

## I. Market state — GTM is the hiring priority
- **GTM is the #1 hiring focus at Anthropic & OpenAI.** Anthropic enterprise customers ≈80% of its ~$47B annualized run-rate; >1,000 businesses spend $1M+/yr (doubled in ~2 months). **[strong]** — gtmnow.com/anthropic-and-openai-are-hiring-gtm-roles-more-than-anything-else, thegtmnewsletter.substack.com
- **Three most-hired GTM roles at labs:** Forward-Deployed Engineers (FDE), Enterprise AEs, Solutions Architects. **[strong]** — bettsrecruiting.com/blog/top-10-gtm-roles-in-ai-for-2026
- **FDE postings grew ~800% Jan–Sep 2025**; 224 open FDE roles across 39 AI cos by 2026 (Palantir 51, OpenAI 31, Databricks 12, Mistral 11, Cohere 10, Scale 8). **[strong]** — jobsbyculture.com/blog/forward-deployed-engineer-boom-2026

## II. What hiring managers screen for
- **Credibly discuss technical concepts AND translate to business value** — bridging technical/business is non-negotiable; standout candidates pair technical depth with sales/CS/marketing. **[strong]** — bettsrecruiting.com, skaled.com
- **Triage > perfection (FDE):** "FDEs who try to rewrite everything create delays. FDEs who triage effectively get hired." Link deployments to retention. **[strong]** — fde.academy, jobsbyculture.com
- **T-shaped talent**: deep in one area + broad fluency; technical fluency via tinkering > formal credentials; commercial mindset; operationalizing automation. **[strong]** — clay.com/blog/gtm-engineering, thegtme.com
- **Soft skills now weigh more than hard credentials** — 92% of hiring managers say soft skills matter more than ever in 2026; skills-based hiring replacing credential screens. **[strong]** — skaled.com, herohunt.ai

## III. The builder + seller hybrid (Bill's exact profile)
- **Hybrid "builder-seller" is highly valued, NOT seen as unfocused.** The "AI GTM Engineer" is explicitly half commercial thinker, half builder. **[strong]** — clay.com, origamiagents.com
- **"Master both traditional GTM AND AI-native execution → $200K–$500K+ packages. Know only one → already behind."** Window of advantage closes ~mid-2026. **[strong]** — superinterviews.substack.com
- **Founder/early-stage experience is valued** (founder, VC, early employee). "Trajectory" is the most reliable signal, not pedigree. Preferred: closed complex deals + quota, OR built a GTM motion from scratch as first/early GTM hire. **[moderate→strong]** — modelexpand.com (Kleiner Perkins talent guide), joinrise.co
- **Distribution is the moat as models commoditize** — enterprise relationships + human-led complex procurement are the defensible advantage. Position as a **"distribution builder"** who gets AI product + customer relationships. **[strong]** — gtmnow.com, superinterviews.substack.com

## IV. Demonstrated work / portfolio beats credentials
- "Recruiters don't hire potential — they hire proof." End-to-end demos +40% callbacks; showing collaboration history +2.4x interview rate. **[strong]** — blockchain-council.org, artificialintelligencejobs.co.uk
- For GTM: **3–5 artifacts with measured outcomes** (pipeline generated, time saved, conversion lift) — actual work product, not resume bullets. "Employers expect receipts." **[strong]** — superinterviews.substack.com
- For technical roles: clean GitHub + deployed projects (Streamlit/APIs/Docker) > notebooks; explain what/why/how-to-run in 30s. **[strong]** — precisionaiacademy.com

## V. Red flags / mistakes
- **Player-coach trap:** don't expect one leader to both carry quota AND build the function — "Both are full-time jobs" (Kleiner Perkins' Lindsey Keller). **[moderate]**
- GTM-engineer failure modes: tunnel vision (one channel), shiny-object syndrome, over-engineering, no commercial awareness, email-infra gaps (SPF/DKIM/DMARC), can't define ICP fast. **[moderate]** — heyreach.io, thegtme.com
- Employment gaps becoming normalized (52% had ≥1-month gap in 2025) but still a manager concern (reliability/retention). Address trajectory directly. **[strong]** — livecareer.com

## VI. What separates winners (the three differentiators)
1. **Dual competency** — traditional GTM (positioning, partnerships, messaging) + AI-native execution (agentic workflows, automation, measuring AI impact).
2. **Demonstrated building** — shipped automations/workflows/integrations with measurable results.
3. **Agentic-workflow judgment** — when to automate vs. keep humans in the loop; designing guardrails.
**[strong]** — superinterviews.substack.com
- FDE interview decomposition: clarify before solving, break into components, propose MVP first, discuss trade-offs, think aloud. Managers prioritize ownership + structured problem-solving + shipping, not memorized knowledge. **[strong]** — hashnode.com
