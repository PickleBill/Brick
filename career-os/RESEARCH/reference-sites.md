## Synthesized Patterns

### 1. Above-the-fold — clarity in <10 seconds
- **First-person, one-sentence thesis** beats a job title. The strongest name *what they do for whom*, not their title. **[strong]**
- **Headline formula:** `[first-person verb] + [specific outcome] + [for whom]`. Julian compresses to claim + qualifying question, which self-selects the right visitor and implies confidence. **[strong]**
- **Falsifiable claim = a number or a named entity in the first viewport.** Vague adjectives ("strategic, results-driven") are absent from every strong site. The claim must be checkable. **[strong]**
- For Bill's "sells AND builds" thesis, pair a GTM number with a build artifact in the same breath — the site itself being the build. **[speculation]**

### 2. Proof — making work feel real, not gimmicky
- **Per-item quantified metrics, attached to the specific thing** (Brittany puts "100k+ installs" on the project, not a banner). Metrics feel real when bound to an artifact, abstract when floated in a hero counter. **[strong]**
- **External verification links** (Product Hunt, GitHub, named clients) let skeptics audit. Proof you can click beats proof you must trust. **[strong]**
- **Failure/abandonment visibility paradoxically raises credibility** (Levels' "7% success rate"). **[moderate]** — powerful but tonally risky; use selectively.
- **Curate, don't dump.** Show the best 3–5, offer "view full archive" as a secondary path. **[strong]**
- **Craft-as-proof:** for anyone selling building ability, the site's own polish + visible build stack *is* the case study. **[strong]** — directly relevant to "the artifact demonstrates the claim."
- **Depth write-ups:** one fully narrated case study (problem → moves → outcome) outperforms ten logos for senior/operator roles. **[moderate]**

### 3. Personality vs credibility — distinctive without "AI-slop"
- **Anchor personality to 1–2 specific true details, not a vibe.** Specificity reads human; generic warmth reads generated. **[strong]**
- **Stated values + explicit anti-values** project discernment and are hard to fake-generate. **[moderate]**
- **One restrained easter egg, not pervasive whimsy.** **[strong]**
- **Longevity/maintenance signals** ("1999–2026," commit hash, live status) counter the throwaway feel of AI-generated sites. **[moderate]**
- Anti-slop throughline: **specificity, restraint, checkability.** AI-slop is generic, over-animated, unfalsifiable; these sites are the inverse. **[strong]**

### 4. CTAs
- **Primary CTA is singular and audience-qualifying.** Name the *one* action for the *one* visitor that matters. **[strong]**
- **Contact low-friction, not a form wall.** Copy-email-with-feedback or direct mailto beats forms for relationship-driven senior roles. **[strong]**
- **Secondary CTAs are exploratory, not competing** ("read my writing," "view archive"). **[strong]**
- For Bill: primary CTA qualifies a hiring manager/partner (direct warm contact); the terminal/build is the proof-browsing secondary. **[speculation]**

### 5. Structure / IA
- **One scroll page or shallow hub-and-spoke**, depth one click down. **[strong]**
- **Section order:** thesis/hero → short about → experience/proof → selected work → writing/thought-leadership → contact. **[strong]**
- **Nav minimal** — 4–6 items + social. **[moderate]**
- **Multiple entry points for different audiences** without separate sites. **[moderate]**
- **Mobile:** content-first, fast, readable; signature animations degrade gracefully. **[strong]**

### 6. Signature moments
- **One memorable interaction, tightly scoped**, often tied to function not decoration. **[strong]**
- **The signature should reinforce the claim.** For Bill, an interactive terminal that answers grounded questions *is* the "AI-native, sells-and-builds" claim made tangible. **[speculation, strongly supported by the craft-as-proof pattern]**
- **Restraint over spectacle.** Energy concentrated in 1–2 moments against an otherwise calm, fast canvas — this reconciles "keep the color/motion energy" with the failed all-muted de-slop pass: keep *concentrated* energy, not ambient motion everywhere. **[strong]**

## PATTERNS BILL SHOULD ADOPT
1. **Lead with a first-person, falsifiable thesis in the first viewport** — pair a GTM outcome (number) with a build artifact (this site) so "sells AND builds" is provable on sight. [strong]
2. **Use a claim + qualifying question** to self-select the frontier-lab hiring manager/partner. [moderate]
3. **Attach every metric to its specific artifact** rather than a floating stats banner. [strong]
4. **Make claims checkable** — link to real evidence; keep the FACTS.md-backed ledger so nothing on a surface is unverifiable. [strong]
5. **Curate to the best 3–5 stories/projects**, then a "full archive" secondary path. [strong]
6. **Treat the site itself as the headline case study** — polished build + a "how this was built" note. [strong]
7. **Build a Levels-style track-record view**: dated, status-tagged items with selective honesty. [moderate — calibrate to GTM tone]
8. **The terminal is the signature moment** — one concentrated, on-brand interaction that *is* the AI-native claim; keep the rest calm. [strong-by-analogy]
9. **Anchor personality to 2–3 specific true details + explicit values/anti-values.** [strong]
10. **Ship exactly one restrained delight moment**, tied to function (e.g., a terminal command). [strong]
11. **Singular, audience-qualifying primary CTA + frictionless contact**; exploratory secondary CTAs that don't compete. [strong]
12. **Shallow IA**: one primary scroll/hub, 4–6 nav items, depth one click down. [strong]
13. **Concentrate motion energy in 1–2 moments**; everything else fast, calm, readable. [strong]
14. **Longevity/maintenance signals** ("last updated," live status). [moderate]
15. **Show one fully narrated GTM case study** (problem → moves → measured outcome). [moderate]

## ANTI-PATTERNS TO AVOID
- Vague adjective soup ("strategic, results-driven, passionate"). [strong]
- Floating vanity stat banners disconnected from any artifact. [strong]
- Exhaustive dumps with no curation hierarchy. [strong]
- Ambient over-animation. [strong]
- Contact-form walls for senior relationship-driven outreach. [moderate]
- Deep multi-page IA that buries the thesis. [moderate]
- Unfalsifiable claims with nothing to click through to — especially corrosive for "sells AND builds." [strong]
- Generic-template personality (stock "fun facts," emoji garnish). [strong]
- Copying any site's copy/structure verbatim. [strong]
