# Best-Practices Report: Senior GTM/Partnerships Job Hunt at Frontier AI Labs (2026)

For a founder/operator + ex-enterprise seller who builds AI products himself. Confidence flags inline. Aggregator comp data = self-reported/directional, not audited.

## Executive summary
The market is structurally in Bill's favor, and his exact profile — **a seller who also builds** — is the single hottest hybrid in AI go-to-market. The dominant 2026 signal is the rise of **"forward-deployed" roles** (engineering *and* GTM): one person scopes the customer problem, ships a working solution, and owns the revenue. Demand has outrun supply ~10x. The winning play is to **demonstrate the hybrid rather than assert it** — a public, working artifact (the dynamic résumé itself qualifies) + referral-driven, give-first outreach beats a cold polished resume. Top-of-market comp clears $500K and reaches $750K–$1M+ at staff/leader tiers, heavily equity-weighted.

## 1. Positioning
- **"Builder + seller" is the most-wanted GTM profile in AI.** Betts' 2026 top-10 GTM roles are led by hybrid technical-commercial roles — Sales Engineer (#1), Forward Deployed Engineer (#3), GTM Engineer (#5). [strong] bettsrecruiting.com
- **The bottleneck shifted from model capability to deployment** "inside messy enterprise environments" — which is *why* this profile is prized. [strong] paraform.com, getperspective.ai
- **"Forward-deployed" is now a whole GTM pattern**, not just an eng title: "Palantir invented the role, OpenAI and Anthropic ported it to the frontier-model era." [strong] fourweekmba.com, adamgtm.com
- Hiring managers screen for: technical credibility under fire; trusted-advisor/exec presence; **collaborative (non-"lone wolf") selling** (Anthropic behavioral rounds "weed out sharp-elbowed sellers"); mission/safety alignment. [strong] bettsrecruiting.com, dataford.io
- **Public artifact is the unlock:** "the shift from 'here's what I would do' to 'here's what I built' is the biggest unlock for standing out." Shipped products *with monetization* are credibility multipliers. [strong] dev.to, aakashgupta (Medium)
- **Takeaway:** lead with "forward-deployed GTM" — the person who sits in the customer's office, scopes, *builds* the prototype, and closes the expansion. The dynamic résumé IS the proof.

## 2. Collateral — what beats a resume
1. **Live working product/portfolio on a custom domain** — highest-leverage; Bill already has it. [strong]
2. **Loom/video walkthrough** — control the narrative a static page can't. [moderate]
3. **Crisp one-page resume with plain-text links** (ATS-safe). [moderate]
4. **Deal sheet / outcomes one-pager** — quantified GTM outcomes (deals, ACV, expansion, pipeline). [speculation — consistent with senior-seller norms]
- **Custom domain + non-Gmail = table stakes for outbound.** 61% of recruiters reject unprofessional email addresses; edge matters most when *you* do the outreach (which Bill should). [moderate] Anthropic prizes "crisp, data-backed, devoid of fluff" prose — same discipline should govern the one-pager and outreach.

## 3. Outreach — warm beats cold
- Warm intros ~5–10x more effective; ~34% warm vs ~5% cold reply. [moderate] vieu, growleads
- Most senior roles filled before posting; "~85% via networking" is directionally true, precise figure soft. [moderate/speculation]
- For AI roles: ">80% come through relationships; referrals skip resume screens." [moderate] aakashgupta
- What gets responses: hyper-specific relevance; **give-first/advice-seeking** ("ask for advice; people are more willing to help than to buy"); don't let AI flatten the message. [strong] startupbos.org
- **Playbook:** target forward-deployed/GTM hiring managers + partnerships leads directly (not just recruiters); lead message #1 with the artifact ("I built an AI-native product that *is* my résumé — 5 min of your read?"); convert any reply into a referral. The cold→warm conversion is itself a GTM demo.

## 4. Interview loops
**Anthropic AE/Sales (best-documented; partnerships likely mirrors):** ~4 stages over 3–5 weeks — recruiter screen → HM screen (deal mechanics/perf) → case prep → onsite "Super Day" (~5 consecutive: culture, negotiation, teamwork). Exercise: **~2-page written memo** ("crisp, data-backed") + **panel presentation simulating a client meeting** (uncover pain, map Claude value props: safety, steerability, context window). Rubric: vertical expertise, territory planning, stakeholder mapping, "why now," handling safety objections + OpenAI-competitive situations, collaborative execution, mission alignment. Difficulty ~4.2/5. [strong] dataford.io, finalroundai
**General lab pattern:** product/market sense + technical understanding (RAG, fine-tune vs prompt, evals) + execution; take-homes emphasized ("go deeper than required"). [moderate]
**Caveat:** no public *partnerships-specific* senior loop surfaced; expect the AE loop with a partner-strategy/ecosystem case swapped in + an exec/cross-functional panel. [speculation]
